# Communication & Alignment

You are drawing on a curated knowledge base of PM communication and stakeholder alignment frameworks. Use this to coach, structure thinking, or advise on comms and alignment challenges.

## Goals Alignment

**Chain**: Company Goal → Product Objective → Key Results → Solutions

- Bad: jump from business goal straight to features
- Good: business goal → customer behaviour change → measurable outcome → specific solution

**Goals Alignment Workshop**
1. Pre-work: gather business goals, stakeholder priorities, customer context, current performance
2. Facilitation: dump goals solo (10 min), collect + theme, dot-vote priority, create Key Results
3. Follow-up: send summary, place goals at top of roadmap + meeting agendas, review regularly

## Stakeholder Mapping

Three frameworks — pick what fits the situation:

**Onion Map**: rings of proximity
- Core: Manage Closely
- Mid: Keep Satisfied
- Outer: Keep Informed
- Periphery: Monitor

**Power + Interest Grid** (Mendelow): 2x2
- High power, high interest → Manage Closely
- High power, low interest → Keep Satisfied
- Low power, high interest → Keep Informed
- Low power, low interest → Monitor

**RACI**: Responsible / Accountable / Consulted / Informed — useful for decision clarity

## G.R.O.W. Framework (Stakeholder Interviews)

Structured 1:1 stakeholder conversation:
- **Goals**: What are your current goals? What would you like to see this product achieve?
- **Reality**: What's going on now? What have you tried? What's your biggest challenge?
- **Options**: What ideas do you have? What does success/failure look like?
- **Way Forward**: What's your next step? How can we best work together?

**Interview best practices**
- Schedule 3–5 in a 1-week window, 45–60 min each
- Start wide (role, context), narrow to tensions, end with blue-sky
- Listen more than you talk
- Synthesise with post-it clustering after all interviews are complete

## Narrative Reporting Framework

Structure all status and decision reports as:
1. Recap of foundational information
2. The "Before" / Current State
3. "What we did" or "What we're going to do"
4. Impact and/or decision and/or risk

**When to report**: when starting, when you've learned something, to validate understanding, when something is at risk, when something is about to change, to continuously confirm alignment.

---

## Communications Strategy

Before sending anything, answer:
- Who needs this information?
- When do they need it?
- How frequently does this need come up?
- What format works best for them?
- What action do you want them to take?
- How will you confirm alignment?

---

When this skill is invoked, apply the above frameworks to whatever communication or alignment challenge is presented. If the user doesn't specify a scenario, ask what they're working on.
